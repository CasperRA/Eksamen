Xenos

Car Brand Project

Brand identity, Content Manangement & Coding

Made for mobile and desktop

Xenos was a 2 part project about making a car brand and website for it, the first part consisted of us making a brand, design, website for mobile and starting to focus on the business part of our projects.
The Second part was more so a continuation of some feedback and to further develop the concept of Xenos with more content.

My main role for the first part of this project was being responsible for finding a brand identity and the customers that would interact with it.

To start the project of, we did a lot of research about different car brands, to find what kind of identity we wanted. We ended on being a brand leaning towards being more of a trust worthy, electric mass market car company, with our main customer segments being people that are either, environmentally conscious, craftsmen or early to middle aged family parrents.

Now that we had a clear vision of what our brand should include, we started on our design and after settling down on a more neutral look, we began sketching, wireframing, making mockups and finally coding.

In the coding part I was responsible for creating a website for craftsmen and making our pullout menu to connect all of our webpages together.

We then had a showcase of our work from part 1 and after getting feedback, it was back to the drawingboard to begin part 2.

In part 2 I was responsible for making our contentplan, deciding on what kind of content should be put out for our customers, here I created a webpage with the current campaigns from Xenos on it. Updated the menu and some other pages to have more cohesiveness.

Finally at the end we made the website responsive to desktop, where we added more whitespace and could get a bit more creative with some of our design decisions.
